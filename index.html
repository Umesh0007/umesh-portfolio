About

Engineering background aligned with resilient design and advanced research

I completed an MSc in Civil and Structural Engineering at Teesside University, where my thesis compared residential building design in high seismic zones across India and Italy using software-based structural modeling and code comparison. I also hold a B.Tech in Civil Engineering from GITAM, where I studied the behavior of a G+6 reinforced concrete building under different Indian seismic zones.

My current role in construction engineering strengthens my practical understanding of site inspection, compliance, structural integrity review, and quality assurance.

Research Focus

From seismic safety to Mars habitat systems

Mars Habitat Research

Habitable systems for the Olympus Mons region

Concept development around Mars settlement challenges including low oxygen, radiation, gravity, resource availability, logistics, dust and regolith, and long-term sustainability.

  • Underground or regolith-shielded dome habitat strategies
  • Environmental and soil analysis before foundation decisions
  • Power, life support, waste management, and robotic construction planning
ISRU Systems

Resource extraction and closed-loop support systems

Research interest in oxygen, water, methane, and construction materials produced from Martian atmosphere and regolith, inspired by MOXIE-style oxygen generation and sustainable mission logistics.

  • CO₂-to-O₂ conversion concepts
  • Water ice and regolith-based construction pathways
  • Supply chain reduction through local resource utilization
Geotechnical Simulation

Advanced computational mechanics

Emerging publication themes include MOOSE-based geotechnical simulation, two-phase contact algorithms in MPM for saturated soil-structure interaction, and geotechnology for sustainable infrastructure.

  • Multiphysics modeling workflows
  • Hydro-mechanical interaction simulation
  • Parallel computing in engineering analysis
