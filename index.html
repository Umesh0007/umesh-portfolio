Advanced Geotechnical Simulations Using the MOOSE Framework

This work explores the MOOSE framework for geotechnical simulations, focusing on transient and quasi-static analysis. It covers model setup, strain and stress evaluation, boundary fixation, initial stress conditions, construction staging, mesh handling, and advanced material behavior. The study emphasizes robust input management, unit consistency, and integration with external libraries to improve efficiency and simulation accuracy in large-scale geotechnical design.

🌍

Geotechnology Applications in Civil Engineering

This paper examines how geotechnology supports modern civil engineering through soil stabilization, geospatial mapping, remote sensing, and GIS-assisted infrastructure planning. It highlights applications in earthquake-resistant design, landslide assessment, foundation engineering, and sustainable development, showing how digital tools can improve construction safety, efficiency, and environmentally responsible practice.

🚀

Mars Habitat Research

This study reviews the major environmental and engineering challenges of creating habitable systems on Mars. It addresses atmospheric composition, severe temperature variation, low pressure, reduced gravity, radiation exposure, dust hazards, and the need for long-term sustainability. It also discusses possible solutions such as oxygen generation, greenhouse-gas-based environmental modification, underground or shielded habitats, and use of Martian minerals and regolith.

🔬

Revolutionizing Geotechnical Simulations

This paper introduces a two-phase contact algorithm within the Material Point Method for complex saturated soil-structure interaction. The proposed framework addresses stability issues, pressure oscillations, and stiffness differences between soil and rigid materials. By incorporating semi-implicit integration and improved contact handling, the method enables larger stable time steps and more accurate simulation of hydro-mechanical interaction in geotechnical systems.
